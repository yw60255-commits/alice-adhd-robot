\# TTS Comparison



\## Objective

Compare available Text-to-Speech services and choose the most suitable voice output for an ADHD-friendly interaction flow.



\## Tested Services

\- Minimax TTS

\- Edge TTS

\- QWEN TTS (reference only, if available)



\## Test Criteria

\- Naturalness

\- Clarity

\- Emotional tone

\- Calmness

\- Speed / latency

\- Suitability for child-friendly interaction



\## Test Text A

你好，我们现在开始今天的任务。我会慢慢陪你完成，不用着急。先听我说完第一步。



\## Test Text B

我们现在要换到下一个步骤了。先深呼吸一下，我会继续陪着你。



\## Results Table

| TTS Service | Naturalness | Clarity | Emotion | Speed | Suitable for ADHD Scenario | Notes |

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Minimax TTS | High | High | High | Medium | Yes | Good for calm and supportive tone |

| Edge TTS | Medium | Good | Low | Fast | Partly | Good backup due to speed |

| QWEN TTS | High | Good | Medium | Unknown | Possibly | Not the main workshop option |



\## Summary

Minimax TTS performed best in emotional expression and calm delivery.

Edge TTS responded faster and can serve as a backup solution when low latency is needed.



\## Final Decision

\- Main TTS: Minimax TTS

\- Backup TTS: Edge TTS



\## Reason for Selection

Minimax TTS is selected as the main TTS service because it produces more natural, calm, and expressive voice output, which better supports ADHD-oriented interaction design.
